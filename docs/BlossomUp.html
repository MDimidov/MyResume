Detailed Analysis of Cognitive Dimensions
Pattern Recognition
94%
Pattern Recognition involves recognizing, identifying, and categorizing complex sensory arrangements into organized patterns that aid in memory storage and retrieval.

This process is both spontaneous and automatic, defining your ability to discern order in chaotic settings. Pattern recognition is closely associated with your overall level of intelligence, as it is crucial for logical thinking and the ability to understand and interpret logical sequences.

The "Pattern Recognition" set of questions is designed to assess how swiftly and effortlessly you can perceive the underlying mechanics of various scenarios and identify connections between different elements.
Visual Perception
91%
Visual Perception is the visual-cognitive skill that enables us to organize, process, and interpret visual information, allowing us to understand and make sense of what we see.

The assessment of your visual perception skills measures how keenly you can detect minor variations in the forms and shapes of objects and how precisely you can identify similarities or differences based on size, color, or dimensions.

Visual perception is a crucial component of human intelligence and an essential skill in the learning process. It is thought that individuals with highly developed visual perception are often quicker and more effective learners.
Spatial Orientation
100%
Spatial Orientation is the cognitive skill that enables you to comprehend spatial relationships, specifically the ability to recognize, visualize, and understand the orientation of objects in space and their various interactions within a particular environment.

The questions designed to evaluate your Spatial Orientation skills aim to measure the clarity with which you perceive spatial configurations and the positions or movements of objects within them.

In this context, the Spatial Orientation question set tests your "raw intelligence," which refers to cognitive abilities that do not depend on previously acquired knowledge. Consequently, your performance on this assessment is a crucial indicator of your overall intelligence.
Analytical Thinking
90%
Analytical Thinking involves the ability to dissect information into smaller components, then analyze and assess these parts to develop logical solutions to problems or make reasoned decisions based on evidence.

It is highly correlated with both the ability to identify problems and the capacity to solve them. Your skills in Analytical Thinking dictate how swiftly and effectively you can recognize issues and devise suitable solutions.

Your score on the Analytical Thinking assessment reflects how effectively you can analyze information and make informed decisions, skills that are deemed essential for success in academic, professional, and personal spheres.
Abstract Reasoning
86%
Abstract Reasoning involves the capability to handle abstract concepts and grasp new ideas independently of previously acquired knowledge.

The "Abstract Reasoning" question set assesses your overall potential for intelligence without the influence of your educational background, social environment, or life experiences on the outcomes.

These questions aim to gauge your "fluid intelligence", which is your capacity to rapidly discern logical patterns, trends, and rules in new information and utilize this understanding to address complex issues.

Studies indicate that assessments of abstract reasoning are highly accurate in predicting an individual's potential success in future job roles, and are commonly utilized by HR departments during the candidate screening process.

Gifted learners often possess the ability to learn quickly and retain information with little repetition. They thrive in environments that present intellectually challenging and creative tasks. After you complete the IQ Test, your responses are evaluated against the results of others in your age group who have previously taken the test, and a normalized score is then calculated.

This normalization process sets the average IQ score at 100. Your score indicates how you compare to your peers in the same age group who have also taken the test.

IQ scores are distributed normally across the population. About 95% of people have scores within two standard deviations (SD) from the mean, which places them in a range from 70 to 130, and 98% score below 131.

It's important to note that IQ points are not percentage points. While a standard deviation represents a 15-point difference, and two standard deviations equal a 30-point difference, this doesn't mean that an IQ of 50 is half the cognitive ability of an IQ of 100.

The answer is NO; it's not that straightforward. While intelligence forms a solid basis for success, your IQ score alone doesn't convey much without context. Understanding yourself goes beyond just knowing your IQ score.
